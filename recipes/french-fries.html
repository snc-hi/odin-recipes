French Fries

Description

French fries originated either from Belgium or France. They are prepared by cutting the potato into even strips, then drying and frying it, usually in a deep fryer.

They are served hot, either soft or crispy, and are generally eaten as part of lunch or dinner or by themselves as a snack. They are often salted and may be served with ketchup, vinegar, mayonnaise, tomato sauce, or other local specialties.

Ingredients (Servings: 2)

  • 1 russet potato, cut into evenly sized strips
  • 1 quart vegetable oil for frying
  • salt to taste
